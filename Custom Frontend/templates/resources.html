Combat Sea Level Rise

Reduce Climate Change Through Carbon Emissions

The primary driver of sea level rise is climate change, which causes thermal expansion of water and melting of polar ice caps. To curb sea level rise, we need to reduce global greenhouse gas emissions.

Protecting and Restoring Natural Ecosystems

Wetlands and salt marshes act as natural barriers, absorbing floodwaters and reducing storm surges. Protecting and restoring these ecosystems can help buffer coastal areas from rising seas.

Additionally, planting trees and restoring forests can help absorb carbon dioxide and reduce the greenhouse effect, slowing climate change.
